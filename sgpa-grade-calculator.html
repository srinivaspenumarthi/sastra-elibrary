Understanding CGPA: Complete Guide for Sastra Students

What is CGPA?

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) represents your overall academic performance across all semesters at Sastra University. It's calculated by dividing total grade points earned by total credits attempted, providing a comprehensive measure of your academic achievement.

CGPA vs SGPA

While SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) measures performance in a single semester, CGPA provides a comprehensive view of your entire academic journey at Sastra University. CGPA considers all semesters and gives employers a complete picture of your consistency.

Sastra Grading System

Sastra University follows a 10-point grading scale: S=10 (Outstanding), A+ =9 (Excellent), A=8 (Very Good), B=7 (Good), C=6 (Average), D=5 (Below Average), and F=2 (Fail). This standardized system ensures fair evaluation across all departments.

CGPA Calculation Formula

CGPA = (Sum of all Semester Grade Points) ÷ (Total Credits of all Semesters)

Example: If you have completed 4 semesters with 20 credits each and SGPAs of 8.5, 8.2, 8.8, and 8.0, your CGPA would be: (8.5×20 + 8.2×20 + 8.8×20 + 8.0×20) ÷ (20+20+20+20) = 8.375
